# Product

## Register

brand

## Users

Mixed patient base: general patients seeking a reliable local dentist (nervous, reassurance-driven), cosmetic patients pursuing smile makeovers and veneers (aspirational, aesthetics-led), and emergency patients needing quick confidence in a practice. All arrive via the landing page. The design must signal quality to the aspirational buyer without alienating the everyday patient.

## Product Purpose

Marketing site for Dr Deesha Dental — a dental practice positioning itself as precise, trustworthy, and polished. Success means a prospective patient reads the brand as professional and credible within seconds, then books an appointment. The photography grid of real smiles does the persuasion work; the interface frames it without getting in the way.

## Brand Personality

Clean. Authoritative. Unhurried.

Clinical precision expressed through restraint, not sterility. The practice feels expert without feeling cold. Confidence is communicated by what is left out as much as what is included.

## Anti-references

- **Loud/salesy dental sites**: discount banners, "BOOK TODAY" urgency, promo-first layouts, neon CTAs.
- **Dark/moody aesthetics**: black backgrounds, dramatic shadows, heavy contrast that reads as gothic or oppressive rather than premium.
- **Generic dental stock**: teal-and-white color palettes, clip-art tooth logos, before/after sliders as hero content.

## Design Principles

1. **Restraint is the message.** Every element earns its presence. If it does not serve the patient's trust or the booking action, remove it.
2. **The work speaks first.** Photography of real patient smiles carries the brand. Typography and layout frame it; they do not compete with it.
3. **Precision over decoration.** Sharp spacing, exact hierarchy, deliberate motion. Craft in the details signals craft in the dental work.
4. **Light and confident.** The palette and layouts should feel bright, airy, and certain — never heavy, dramatic, or anxious.
5. **Broad welcome.** Accessible to a nervous first-timer and an informed cosmetic patient alike. No jargon. No intimidation.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

WCAG AA. Adequate contrast on all text and interactive elements. All animations respect `prefers-reduced-motion`. Keyboard-navigable interactive elements.
